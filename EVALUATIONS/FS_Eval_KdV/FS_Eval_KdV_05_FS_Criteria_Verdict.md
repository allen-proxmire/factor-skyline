## 1. Minimality

**Question:** Do the KdV axioms form a minimal architectural set?

### 1.1 Assessment of Individual Axioms

**KdV-1 (Real Scalar Field).** *Minimal.* The real-valued state u(x, t) is the defining choice of state variable. Replacing with a complex field gives NLS — a different architecture. **Minimal.**

**KdV-2 (Locality).** *Minimal.* Defines the PDE paradigm. **Minimal.**

**KdV-3 (Third-Order Dispersion).** *Minimal.* The third-order derivative u_{xxx} is the *defining dispersive term*. It is the unique odd-order, isotropic, linear dispersive operator of lowest nontrivial order (first-order partial_x is transport, not dispersion; third-order is the first genuinely dispersive odd-order operator). Replacing u_{xxx} with a second-order term gives a diffusive equation; removing it gives Burgers. **Minimal — this is the KdV's dispersive identity.**

**KdV-4 (Quadratic Advection).** *Minimal.* The term 6u u_x is the simplest nonlinearity compatible with the KdV integrability structure. The coefficient 6 is a normalization convention (can be rescaled to any nonzero constant without changing the structure). The *quadratic* power u u_x (not u^2 u_x, not u u_{xx}) is *uniquely required* by the bi-Hamiltonian structure — changing the power breaks integrability. **Minimal — forced by the integrability requirement.**

**KdV-5 (Hamiltonian Structure).** *Derived from KdV-3 + KdV-4.* The Hamiltonian structure i psi_t = delta H / delta psi* (in the first formulation u_t = partial_x (delta H_2 / delta u)) is a *consequence* of the specific combination of u_{xxx} and u u_x — it does not need to be imposed separately. **Redundant (derived).**

**KdV-6 (Infinite Conservation Laws).** *Derived from KdV-5.* The infinite conservation laws are generated by the bi-Hamiltonian structure through the Lenard recursion. They are *consequences* of the KdV equation's algebraic structure, not independent axioms. **Redundant (derived).**

**KdV-7 (Complete Integrability / IST).** *Derived from KdV-3 + KdV-4.* The Lax pair L = -partial_{xx} + u, A = -4 partial_{xxx} + 6u partial_x + 3u_x satisfies L_t = [A, L] *because* u solves the KdV equation. The integrability is a *consequence* of the specific PDE, not an additional axiom. **Redundant (derived — the deepest derived property).**

**KdV-8 (1D Euclidean).** *Structural.* The KdV is intrinsically one-dimensional — the integrability, soliton structure, and bi-Hamiltonian framework are all 1D properties. The restriction to R^1 is not a simplification of a higher-dimensional equation but the *natural domain* of the KdV. Unlike the Euclidean restriction in other architectures (which is a simplification of a general-d equation), the KdV's 1D character is *structural*. **Minimal (structural, not a simplification).**

**KdV-9 (No Diffusion).** *Minimal.* The absence of diffusion is the structural commitment that makes the equation dispersive rather than parabolic. **Minimal.**

**KdV-10 (No Reaction).** *Minimal.* No source/sink terms. Conservative. **Minimal.**

## 2. Locality

**Question:** Is the KdV architecture fully local?

### 2.1 Assessment

Every channel is local:

- **A (Advection):** 6u u_x depends on u and u_x at each point. Local.
- **D_3 (Dispersion):** u_{xxx} depends on u and its first three derivatives at each point. Local.
- **H (Bi-Hamiltonian):** Both operators J_1 = partial_x and J_2 = partial_x^3 + (2/3)(u partial_x + partial_x u) are local differential operators. Local.
- **I (Integrability):** The *KdV PDE* is local. The IST is a nonlocal *analytical tool* (it involves solving a global eigenvalue problem), but it is not a dynamical channel — it does not introduce nonlocal coupling into the PDE. The distinction: the IST is a *structural property* of the architecture, not an operator in the evolution equation.

No elliptic constraint, no Poisson equation, no Green's function, no integral operator in the PDE. The KdV is fully local at both formulation and solution levels.

The KdV has *infinite-speed propagation* (the Airy kernel has support on all of R for any t > 0), similar to FP and NLS. This is a property of the *linear dispersive part*, not a nonlocal coupling.

**Criterion 2 Verdict: PASS.** Fully local at formulation and solution levels. No nonlocal channel. The IST is a structural property, not a nonlocal operator.

## 3. Determinism

**Question:** Does the KdV architecture uniquely determine the future from the initial data?

### 3.1 Assessment

The KdV has *the strongest determinism of any nonlinear PDE in the Atlas*:

- **Local well-posedness:** In H^s(R) for s >= -3/4 (Kenig–Ponce–Vega; Colliander–Keel–Staffilani–Takaoka–Tao). This is the *sharpest local well-posedness threshold* of any nonlinear dispersive PDE — well below L^2 (s = 0).

- **Global well-posedness:** In H^s(R) for all s >= -3/4. Unconditional — no smallness condition, no sign condition, no conditional hypothesis. The proof uses conservation laws (for s >= 0) and the I-method (for -3/4 <= s < 0).

- **Uniqueness:** Unconditional in the energy class. The IST provides an alternative uniqueness proof: the scattering data of the initial datum uniquely determine the scattering data at all later times, which uniquely determine the solution.

- **Continuous dependence:** The solution depends continuously on the initial data in H^s.

- **No blowup of any kind:** The infinite conservation laws control every Sobolev norm → no norm can diverge → no finite-time singularity.

- **Exact solution (IST):** For Schwartz-class (or sufficient-decay) data, the IST provides the *exact* solution for all time — not an approximation, not a numerical solution, but the exact analytical solution. This is the *ultimate determinism*: the solution is not merely "determined" (existence + uniqueness) but *explicitly computed*.
