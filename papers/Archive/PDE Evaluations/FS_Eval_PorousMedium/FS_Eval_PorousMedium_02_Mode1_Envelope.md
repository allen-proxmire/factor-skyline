## Overview

Mode 1 traces the path from the PME axioms (PME-1 through PME-8) to the architectural envelope. The PME envelope is among the *tightest* in the FS Atlas — tighter than NS (which has an open face), tighter than RD (which has multiple open faces), and comparable in tightness to AC and CH. But the PME envelope achieves its tightness through a different mechanism: where AC uses the maximum principle and CH uses fourth-order smoothing + Lyapunov, the PME uses *degeneracy-enforced finite propagation* combined with *entropy dissipation* and *comparison principles*. The result is an architecture whose entire long-time dynamics are captured by a single universal attractor — the Barenblatt profile.

Throughout:

    partial_t u = Delta(u^m),    m > 1,    u >= 0

on R^d or on bounded domains Omega subset R^d with no-flux boundary conditions.

## 1. Forbidden Configurations

### F1. Negative Densities

**Axiom source:** PME-3 (Nonlinear Diffusion), PME-1 (Continuum).

The density u represents a physical quantity (mass density, pressure, concentration) that is intrinsically non-negative. The PME preserves non-negativity structurally: if u_0(x) >= 0, then u(x, t) >= 0 for all t > 0. The proof follows from the comparison principle: u = 0 is a (trivial) sub-solution, so any solution starting above zero remains above zero.

More precisely: the diffusion flux nabla(u^m) = m u^{m-1} nabla u vanishes wherever u = 0. No flux enters the vacuum from the positive-density region, and no mechanism within the architecture can produce negative values. States with u(x, t) < 0 at any point are axiomatically forbidden.

### F2. Nonlocal Diffusion

**Axiom source:** PME-2 (Locality).

The diffusion operator Delta(u^m) is local — it depends on u and its second derivatives at each point. Nonlocal diffusion operators (fractional Laplacian (-Delta)^s u^m, integral kernels, Levy-flight diffusion) are outside the architecture. The PME has no nonlocal channel — no Poisson equation, no Green's function, no pressure-type coupling.

### F3. Linear (Non-Degenerate) Diffusion

**Axiom source:** PME-3 (Nonlinear Diffusion), PME-8 (m > 1).

The diffusion coefficient D(u) = m u^{m-1} vanishes at u = 0 for m > 1. Linear diffusion (D = const, corresponding to m = 1) is forbidden. The degeneracy at u = 0 is not a technical nuisance — it is the *defining structural feature* that produces all of the PME's distinctive properties: finite-speed propagation, free boundaries, and compact support.

Without degeneracy (m = 1), the equation becomes the linear heat equation, which has infinite propagation speed, Gaussian fundamental solutions, and C^{infinity} smoothing. The PME axioms specifically exclude this regime.

### F4. Infinite-Speed Propagation

**Axiom source:** PME-3, PME-8 (Degenerate Diffusion, m > 1).

The degeneracy D(u) → 0 as u → 0 enforces *finite-speed propagation*: if the initial data u_0 has compact support (u_0 = 0 outside a bounded set), then u(t) has compact support for all t > 0. The support boundary — the free boundary Gamma(t) — moves at finite speed.

The propagation speed of the free boundary is bounded:

    |dR/dt| <= C(m, d, M) t^{beta - 1}

where beta = 1/(d(m-1)+2) and M is the total mass. For the Barenblatt solution, the free boundary expands as R(t) = C t^{beta}, which is *decelerating* (beta < 1). No disturbance can propagate faster than the Barenblatt rate.

Infinite-speed propagation — instantaneous influence at arbitrarily distant points — is forbidden. This is a structural improvement over the linear heat equation, AC, and CH, all of which have infinite propagation speed (though with exponentially small amplitude at large distances).

### F5. Reaction Terms

**Axiom source:** PME-7 (No Reaction).

The PME is a *pure diffusion* equation. Reaction terms R(u), source terms f(x,t), and any local growth/decay mechanisms are forbidden. The entire dynamics are generated by the nonlinear diffusion channel alone. Adding a reaction term would produce a reaction-diffusion equation with porous-medium-type diffusion (a member of the RD class), which is a different architecture.

### F6. Forcing Terms

**Axiom source:** PME-7 (No Reaction/Forcing).

External forcing f(x, t) is forbidden. The PME is autonomous — it generates its own dynamics from the initial data and the nonlinear diffusion operator, with no external energy or mass input. This makes the PME a *closed system* in the thermodynamic sense.

### F7. Finite-Time Blowup

**Axiom source:** PME-3, PME-4, PME-7.

The PME has no blowup mechanism. The structural argument:

1. **Mass conservation** (PME-4): ||u(t)||_{L^1} = ||u_0||_{L^1} for all t.
2. **Comparison principle:** u(x,t) <= B(x,t; M) where B is the Barenblatt solution with the same total mass M. The Barenblatt solution has L^{infinity} norm t^{-alpha}, which *decreases* in time.
3. **L^{infinity} bound:** ||u(t)||_{L^{infinity}} <= C(m,d) M^{2/(d(m-1)+2)} t^{-alpha} → 0 as t → infinity.

The maximum of u is *decreasing* in time — not just bounded, but decaying to zero. Blowup is structurally impossible because the architecture has no mechanism to concentrate mass: the diffusion channel only spreads, and there is no reaction channel to accumulate density.

### F8. Oscillations or Patterns

**Axiom source:** PME-7 (No Reaction), PME-3 (pure diffusion).

The PME has no reaction channel, no coupling between species (n = 1), and no forcing. Without these, the architecture cannot produce:

- Limit cycles or sustained oscillations (require reaction dynamics).
- Turing patterns (require n >= 2 + reaction coupling).
- Traveling waves with selected speed (require bistable or monostable reaction).
- Spatiotemporal chaos (require oscillatory kinetics).

The PME dynamics are *monotone*: the entropy integral H(u) = integral phi(u) dx decreases for all convex phi. The solution smooths, spreads, and decays — it never oscillates, concentrates, or forms spatial structure. The dynamics are the *opposite* of pattern formation.

### F9. Non-Conservation (Mass Creation or Destruction)

**Axiom source:** PME-4 (Conservation), PME-7 (No Reaction).

The divergence form partial_t u = div(nabla(u^m)) with no-flux boundary conditions ensures:

    d/dt integral u dx = integral div(nabla(u^m)) dx = integral nabla(u^m) . n dS = 0

Total mass is exactly conserved. No mechanism within the architecture can create or destroy mass. Non-conservation is forbidden both structurally (divergence form) and constitutively (no reaction term).

### F10. Diffuse Interfaces

**Axiom source:** PME-3, PME-8 (Degenerate Diffusion, m > 1).

The interface between {u > 0} and {u = 0} is *sharp* — it is a free boundary Gamma(t) with u jumping from positive values to exactly zero. There is no diffuse transition layer of width O(epsilon) as in AC or CH. The sharpness is a consequence of the degeneracy: the diffusion shuts off at u = 0, preventing the solution from leaking into the vacuum region with exponentially small tails.

Diffuse interfaces — smooth, tanh-like transitions from one state to another over a finite width — are forbidden. The PME interface is a *genuine discontinuity* in the gradient: u is continuous but nabla u may be discontinuous across Gamma(t). This is the geometric signature of degenerate diffusion.

## 2. Necessary Configurations

### N1. Degenerate Diffusion at u = 0

**Source:** PME-3, PME-8.

The diffusion coefficient D(u) = m u^{m-1} vanishes at u = 0 for all m > 1. This degeneracy is a *permanent structural feature* — it holds at every point where u = 0, at every time, for every solution. The vacuum region is *dynamically frozen*: no diffusion occurs there, no information propagates through it, and no mass enters it from the adjacent positive-density region (except through the advancing free boundary).

### N2. Finite-Speed Propagation

**Source:** PME-3, PME-8.

If u_0 has compact support, then u(t) has compact support for all t > 0. The free-boundary radius satisfies:

    R(t) <= C t^{beta},    beta = 1/(d(m-1) + 2)

The propagation speed dR/dt ~ t^{beta-1} is *decelerating*: the free boundary slows down as it expands. For the Barenblatt solution, the bound is saturated — the Barenblatt free boundary expands at exactly the rate C t^{beta}.

This is a *necessary consequence* of the axioms, not a constitutive choice: every PME solution with compactly supported initial data propagates at finite speed. The finite speed is architecturally forced by the degeneracy.

### N3. Free-Boundary Formation

**Source:** PME-3, PME-8.

Every PME solution with compactly supported initial data develops a free boundary Gamma(t) = partial {u(t) > 0}. The free boundary is not prescribed — it is an *emergent geometric feature* determined by the PDE. Its motion law is:

    V_n (normal velocity) determined by the density gradient at the interface

For the Barenblatt solution: Gamma(t) is a sphere of radius R(t) = C t^{beta}.

The free boundary is the PME's *signature geometric structure* — the analogue of interfaces in AC/CH, but qualitatively different because it is *sharp* (not diffuse) and *geometry-dependent* (its motion depends on curvature and the global mass distribution).

### N4. Mass Conservation

**Source:** PME-4.

    integral_Omega u(x, t) dx = integral_Omega u_0(x) dx =: M    for all t >= 0

Exact conservation. The total mass M is an invariant of the dynamics and a parameter of the Barenblatt attractor.

### N5. Self-Similar Spreading (Barenblatt Attractor)

**Source:** PME-3, PME-4, PME-8 (combined).

For initial data u_0 in L^1(R^d) with total mass M > 0, the solution converges to the Barenblatt profile:

    u(x, t) → B(x, t; M) = t^{-alpha} [C - k |x|^2 / t^{2beta}]_+^{1/(m-1)}

in the intermediate-asymptotics sense:

    t^{alpha} u(t^{beta} x, t) → B_*(x; M)    as t → infinity

where B_* is the Barenblatt profile at t = 1 with mass M. The convergence is in L^1, L^{infinity}, and in suitable weighted norms.

The Barenblatt profile is the *unique universal attractor* of the PME: all solutions with the same total mass converge to the same profile, regardless of the shape of the initial data. This is the PME's version of the central limit theorem: just as the Gaussian is the universal attractor for sums of independent random variables (m = 1), the Barenblatt profile is the universal attractor for nonlinear diffusion (m > 1).

### N6. Algebraic Decay Rates

**Source:** N5.

The Barenblatt scaling determines the decay rates:

    ||u(t)||_{L^{infinity}} ~ t^{-alpha},    alpha = d / (d(m-1) + 2)
    ||u(t)||_{L^p} ~ t^{-alpha(1 - 1/p)},   for 1 <= p <= infinity
    R(t) ~ t^{beta},                          beta = 1 / (d(m-1) + 2)

The decay is *algebraic* (power-law), not exponential. This is slower than the exponential decay of the linear heat equation on bounded domains (which has a spectral gap), but the PME on R^d has no spectral gap — the continuous spectrum of the linearized operator reaches zero.

The decay exponents alpha and beta are *universally determined* by m and d alone. They do not depend on the initial data (beyond the total mass M, which sets the amplitude). This universality is the quantitative expression of the Barenblatt attractor property.

### N7. Positivity Preservation

**Source:** PME-3, comparison principle.

If u_0 >= 0, then u(t) >= 0 for all t > 0. Moreover, if u_0 > 0 on an open set, then u(t) > 0 on a possibly larger open set (the support can expand but never contract in the interior). The comparison with the trivial sub-solution u = 0 gives positivity preservation.

### N8. No Oscillations, No Chaos

**Source:** PME-7 (No Reaction), entropy monotonicity.

The PME solution operator is an *L^1 contraction* (see E5 below) and dissipates all convex entropies (see E4). These properties imply:

- No limit cycles (the L^1 distance between any two solutions is non-increasing — orbits cannot recur).
- No chaos (no sensitive dependence — the contraction property gives Lipschitz stability).
- No sustained oscillations (all convex entropy functionals decrease).

The dynamics are *monotonically smoothing*: every measure of disorder or concentration decreases over time. The PME is the *most boring* PDE dynamics in the FS Atlas — and this is a virtue, because it means the architecture is maximally predictable.

### N9. No Pattern Formation

**Source:** PME-7 (n = 1, no reaction).

With one species and no reaction term, the PME cannot form spatial patterns. There is no Turing instability (requires n >= 2), no spatial heterogeneity generation (requires reaction), and no structure creation. The only spatial structure is the free boundary, which is a *geometric* feature (the boundary of the support) rather than a *pattern* (a spatially modulated concentration profile).

### N10. Convergence to Barenblatt for All Integrable Data

**Source:** PME-3, PME-4, PME-8.

The convergence to the Barenblatt profile is *universal*: it holds for all non-negative initial data u_0 in L^1(R^d) with finite mass M > 0, regardless of the shape, symmetry, or regularity of u_0. The convergence is:

- **In L^1:** ||u(t) - B(t; M)||_{L^1} → 0 as t → infinity.
- **In rescaled variables:** t^{alpha} ||u(t) - B(t; M)||_{L^{infinity}} → 0.
- **With rate:** ||u(t) - B(t; M)||_{L^1} <= C t^{-gamma} for some gamma > 0 depending on the moments of u_0.

This universality theorem (due to Friedman–Kamin, Vazquez, Carrillo–Toscani, and others) is the deepest structural result of the PME architecture: the nonlinear diffusion, acting alone, drives *every* finite-mass solution toward a unique universal profile. No initial-data dependence survives in the long-time limit beyond the total mass.

## 3. Envelope Inequalities (E1–E10)

---

**E1. Degenerate Parabolic Inequality**

At the free boundary Gamma(t) = partial {u > 0}:

    u(x, t) = 0    and    partial_t u >= 0    on Gamma(t) (from the positive side)

The free boundary can only *advance* (expand the support) — it cannot retreat. Once a point enters the support {u > 0}, it remains in the support for all later times. This is the *one-sided* character of the free boundary: mass spreads outward, never inward.

**Structural role:** Ensures monotone expansion of the support. The free boundary is an *irreversible* geometric feature.

---

**E2. Finite-Speed Propagation Bound**

For initial data supported in B(0, R_0):

    supp(u(t)) subset B(0, R(t)),    R(t) <= C(m, d, M) (t + t_0)^{beta}

where beta = 1/(d(m-1)+2) and t_0 accounts for the initial support size. The propagation speed is:

    dR/dt <= C t^{beta - 1}    (decelerating)

No disturbance propagates faster than the Barenblatt rate. This is the *architectural speed limit* of the PME.

---

**E3. Barenblatt Scaling Laws**

The self-similar exponents are:

    alpha = d / (d(m-1) + 2)    (decay exponent)
    beta = 1 / (d(m-1) + 2)     (spreading exponent)
    alpha = d beta                (mass conservation constraint: alpha = d beta)

These exponents satisfy the scaling relation:

    d beta + alpha (m-1) = 1    (self-similar constraint)

The exponents are *uniquely determined* by m and d — they are architectural constants, not adjustable parameters. They set the decay rate of the maximum (t^{-alpha}), the spreading rate of the support (t^{beta}), and the relationship between the two.

---

**E4. Entropy Dissipation Inequality**

For any convex function Phi : [0, infinity) → R with Phi(0) = 0:

    d/dt integral Phi(u) dx <= 0

The PME dissipates *all convex entropies*. In particular:

- **L^p norms** (Phi(u) = u^p, p > 1): d/dt ||u||_{L^p}^p <= 0.
- **Boltzmann entropy** (Phi(u) = u log u): d/dt integral u log u dx <= 0.
- **Renyi entropies** (Phi(u) = u^q / (q-1)): monotone decreasing.

The specific dissipation rate for the entropy H(u) = integral u^m dx / (m-1) is:

    dH/dt = -(4m/(m-1)) integral |nabla(u^{(m+1)/2})|^2 dx <= 0

This is an exact identity — the entropy dissipation is a strictly negative definite quadratic form in the gradient of u^{(m+1)/2}.

**Structural role:** The entropy dissipation inequality is the PME's analogue of the Lyapunov identity in AC/CH. It is *stronger* than a single Lyapunov functional because it applies to *all* convex entropies simultaneously. The PME is *entropy-monotone* in the strongest possible sense.

---

**E5. L^1 Contraction Inequality**

For any two solutions u, v of the PME with the same (or different) initial data:

    ||u(t) - v(t)||_{L^1} <= ||u(0) - v(0)||_{L^1}    for all t >= 0

The PME solution operator is a *contraction* on L^1 — it never increases the L^1 distance between any two solutions. This is the strongest stability estimate available: it implies uniqueness, continuous dependence, and asymptotic convergence.

The L^1 contraction is a consequence of the *comparison principle* and the *divergence structure* of the PME. It holds for all m > 1, all dimensions d, and all non-negative initial data.

**Structural role:** The L^1 contraction is the PME's primary stability mechanism. It is strictly stronger than the NS energy inequality (which bounds L^2 norms and may fail for weak solutions) and comparable to the AC/CH Lyapunov structure.

---

**E6. Comparison Principle**

If u_0(x) <= v_0(x) for all x, then u(x, t) <= v(x, t) for all x and t >= 0.

The PME preserves ordering: if one initial density is everywhere below another, it remains below for all time. This is the *parabolic comparison principle* — valid for scalar degenerate parabolic equations.

**Structural role:** The comparison principle provides upper and lower bounds on solutions by comparison with explicit sub- and super-solutions (especially Barenblatt profiles). It is the scalar-equation analogue of the maximum principle, adapted to the degenerate setting.

---

**E7. Free-Boundary Velocity Inequality**

At the free boundary Gamma(t), the normal velocity satisfies (in a weak sense):

    V_n >= 0    (support can only expand)

and, for smooth free boundaries:

    V_n = -m u^{m-2} (nabla u . n)    at Gamma(t)

where n is the outward normal. The velocity depends on the *density gradient at the interface*: steeper gradients produce faster expansion. For the Barenblatt solution:

    V_n = C t^{beta - 1} = C beta R(t) / t    (decelerating)

**Structural role:** Determines the free-boundary motion law. The free boundary is *slaved* to the density gradient — it has no independent dynamics.

---

**E8. Mass Conservation Identity**

    integral_{R^d} u(x, t) dx = M    for all t >= 0

Exact conservation. The total mass M is an invariant of the dynamics. Combined with the L^{infinity} decay (E3), this implies that the support must expand: as the maximum decreases, the mass must spread over a larger region.

---

**E9. No Blowup Inequality**

    ||u(t)||_{L^{infinity}} <= C(m, d) M^{2beta} t^{-alpha}    for all t > 0

The L^{infinity} norm of u *decreases* algebraically. There is no mechanism for concentration or blowup. The bound is sharp — it is saturated by the Barenblatt solution.

**Structural role:** Seals the envelope from above. Combined with positivity (F1) and mass conservation (E8), this gives complete control: 0 <= u(x,t) <= C t^{-alpha} for all (x,t).

---

**E10. Monotone Smoothing Inequality**

The PME solution operator is *order-preserving* and *entropy-decreasing*:

    u_0 <= v_0  =>  u(t) <= v(t)    [comparison, E6]
    H[u(t_2)] <= H[u(t_1)]    for t_2 >= t_1    [entropy monotonicity, E4]
    ||u(t) - v(t)||_{L^1} <= ||u_0 - v_0||_{L^1}    [L^1 contraction, E5]

These three properties together imply that the PME dynamics are *monotone smoothing*: the solution becomes simpler, smoother, and more symmetric over time, converging to the unique Barenblatt profile with the same mass. No oscillation, no complexity generation, no structure formation.

### Envelope Summary

The PME envelope is defined by ten constraints (E1–E10) organized into three tiers:

**Tier 1 — Hard Constraints (exact identities and sharp bounds):**
- E3: Barenblatt scaling laws (exact exponents alpha, beta).
- E5: L^1 contraction (exact, sharp).
- E8: Mass conservation (exact identity).
- E9: No blowup (sharp L^{infinity} bound, saturated by Barenblatt).

**Tier 2 — Structural Properties (unconditional):**
- E1: Free boundary advances only (one-sided).
- E2: Finite-speed propagation (bounded by Barenblatt rate).
- E4: All convex entropies dissipated.
- E6: Comparison principle (order-preserving).
- E7: Free-boundary velocity slaved to gradient.

**Tier 3 — Asymptotic Result:**
- E10: Monotone smoothing (convergence to Barenblatt).

**Every constraint is unconditional.** No constraint is open, conditional, or dimension-dependent (within d >= 1). The PME envelope is *fully closed* with no open faces.

### 4.2 What Makes the PME Envelope Tight

Three structural mechanisms combine to seal the PME envelope:

**Mechanism 1: Degeneracy.** The vanishing diffusion coefficient at u = 0 enforces finite-speed propagation and sharp interfaces. This prevents the solution from developing exponentially small tails (which would make the support non-compact) and confines the mass to a bounded, expanding region.

**Mechanism 2: Entropy dissipation.** The PME dissipates *all* convex entropies — not just one Lyapunov functional, but an entire family. This provides control in every L^p space simultaneously and ensures that the solution monotonically simplifies over time.

**Mechanism 3: L^1 contraction.** The solution operator is a contraction on L^1, providing the strongest possible stability estimate. This ensures uniqueness, continuous dependence, and convergence to the universal attractor.

These three mechanisms are *independent* and *complementary*: degeneracy controls the geometry (support, propagation), entropy dissipation controls the regularity (smoothing, decay), and L^1 contraction controls the stability (uniqueness, convergence). Together, they produce a *uniquely determined* long-time behavior — the Barenblatt profile — for every finite-mass initial datum.

### 4.3 PME as the Purest Diffusion Architecture

The PME is the *purest diffusion architecture* in the FS Atlas:

- **One channel:** Nonlinear diffusion (D_nl). No reaction, no forcing, no coupling.
- **One geometry:** Free boundary Gamma(t). No interfaces, no patterns, no waves.
- **One attractor:** Barenblatt profile B(x,t;M). Universal, unique, explicit.

This simplicity is not a limitation — it is a *structural achievement*. The PME demonstrates that nonlinear diffusion alone, without any reaction or forcing, can produce:

- Nontrivial geometry (free boundaries).
- Nontrivial dynamics (finite-speed propagation, self-similar spreading).
- A complete asymptotic theory (universal convergence to an explicit profile).

No other architecture in the FS Atlas achieves this level of completeness with so few structural resources.
