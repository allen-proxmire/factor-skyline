4. WHETHER alpha OR beta IS BINDING
========================================================================

  For theta_PNTALL:
    Neither alpha nor beta is relevant.  The Ingham relation
    theta = 1 - 1/||A||_infty does not involve A*(sigma).

  For theta_GAPSQUARE:
    ALPHA is binding (not beta) at the optimal sigma.
    alpha dominates at high sigma where B >> A.
    The alpha formula:
      alpha(s) = 4s - 2 + 2(B(1-s) - 1)/(B - A)
    approaches 4s - 2 + 2(1-s) = 2s as B/A -> infinity,
    which is maximized at sigma -> 1.

5. WHETHER THE BOTTLENECK IS NOW IN THE ENERGY PROJECTION
========================================================================

  SHORT ANSWER: NO (for the prime gap exponent).

  For theta_PNTALL (the primary result), the bottleneck is
  ENTIRELY in the LV projection.  A*(sigma) does not appear.

  For theta_GAPSQUARE (the secondary result), the bottleneck
  is SPLIT between the LV and energy projections.  The
  sensitivities d(GS)/d(A) and d(GS)/d(B) are nearly equal,
  meaning improvements to EITHER A or A* would help.

  The energy estimate A*(sigma) = 12/(4sigma - 1) on [5/6, 1)
  from Heath-Brown (1979) has been unchanged for 45 years.
  Improving it would tighten GAPSQUARE but NOT theta_PNTALL.

6. HOW GM RESHAPED P
========================================================================

  The 5D polytope P in (sigma, tau, rho, rho*, s):

  BEFORE GM:
    The face of P in the rho-direction near sigma ~ 0.7 was
    determined by Huxley's LVE and related estimates.
    The binding ||A||_infty = 12/5 = 2.40 was achieved at
    sigma ~ 5/7 = 0.714, the crossover of Ingham and Huxley.

  AFTER GM:
    GM adds new half-spaces to P:
    (a) Three LV constraints in (sigma, tau, rho)
    (b) Twenty iterated LV constraints (K = 1..20)
    (c) Three LVER constraints in full 5D (sigma,tau,rho,rho*,s)

    The GM LVE face replaces the Huxley face for all sigma > 0.70.
    The crossover point shifts from sigma ~ 0.714 (Ingham-Huxley)
    to sigma = 0.700 (Ingham-GM).

    ||A||_infty decreases from 12/5 to 30/13.
    theta_PNTALL decreases from 7/12 to 17/30.

  GEOMETRIC PICTURE:
    In the (sigma, A(sigma)) plane, A(sigma) looks like an inverted V:
    - Rising limb: A(sigma) = 3/(2-sigma) [Ingham, concave up]
    - Peak: A = 30/13 at sigma = 7/10
    - Falling limb: A(sigma) = 15/(5sigma+3) [GM, concave down]
    - Then passes through Ivic, TTY, Pintz bounds as sigma -> 1

    GM shaved the peak from 12/5 down to 30/13.
    The peak is now a CUSP: two curves meeting at a point.
    The next improvement requires either:
    (i) A new LVE that beats GM to the RIGHT of sigma = 0.70
    (ii) A new ZD estimate that beats Ingham to the LEFT of sigma = 0.70
    (iii) Simultaneously improving both sides

  UNCHANGED FACES:
    - The energy face (rho* constraints) is untouched by GM.
    - The coupling face (s constraints) has new GM contributions
      but these don't affect theta_PNTALL directly.
    - The exponent pair hull is enriched by GM but only contributes
      through the Bourgain EP-to-ZD pathway, binding at high sigma.
